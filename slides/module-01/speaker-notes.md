# Module 1 Speaker Notes

These notes correspond to [slides.md](slides.md). Target: **10–15 minutes**.

## Slide 1 — Agents are already here (1:00)

Open with the business issue, not product architecture. Agents already use company data
and tools. Ask: “Can you see, govern, and secure every agent in your environment today?”

## Slide 2 — Why agents change the risk (1:30)

Agents do more than answer questions. They access data, call tools, take action, and scale.
The value and the control requirement come from the same capability.

## Slide 3 — The control gap (2:00)

Pause on the four questions. Most organizations can answer some, but not all. Do not lead
with fear; describe the gap as the barrier between useful pilots and confident scale.

## Slide 4 — Microsoft Agent 365 (2:00)

Use the official Microsoft Learn framing: Agent 365 is the control plane to **observe,
govern, and secure** agents. Keep the story to those three verbs.

## Slide 5 — Identity is the foundation (2:00)

Identity is the hero. Microsoft Entra Agent ID provides purpose-built identity constructs
for agents. A governed identity connects an accountable owner, authorized access, activity
records, policy, and containment.

## Slide 6 — Why Microsoft 365 E7 (2:00)

Land the commercial message clearly: **Agent 365 is included in Microsoft 365 E7.** E7
combines the agent control plane with Microsoft Entra, Defender, Purview, and Microsoft 365
AI capabilities. Confirm current pricing, terms, and regional availability before quoting.

## Slide 7 — The value story (1:30)

Repeat three proof points throughout the workshop: visibility, risk insight, and control.
Later demos should prove each one in the customer's language.

## Slide 8 — The ask (1:00)

Do not ask for a purchase based on slides alone. Offer one easy next step: list the agents
the customer already has, choose one useful example, and try the Agent 365 controls.

Close with: “Where would better visibility and control let you adopt agents faster?”
